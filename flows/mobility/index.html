❖ Installing Mobility

Step 1: Before you Begin

1. Prerequisites

In order to use Mobility, you should first have registered for an ohmage account and installed the phone application. If you haven't already done so, you may begin that process here:

Installation and First Survey

(Note that you only need to install ohmage and log in; you don't have to join a campaign to begin collecting data with Mobility.)

Getting the Mobility App ►

Step 2: Getting the Mobility App

1. Getting Mobility on Google Play

Mobility is available on Google Play at the following address: Mobility on Google Play

Since Mobility gathers information about your modes of activity, the application requires permission to access your location and accelerometer state. The information is kept according to the terms of the ohmage project, specified here: Terms of Service and Privacy Policy

If you have your phone on hand, you may photograph the QR code on the right with scanning software to link you directly to Mobility on Google Play.

2. Mobility Through Ohmage ►

2. Mobility Through Ohmage

Most of your interaction with the Mobility app will occur through the ohmage app -- Mobility is a "support application" for ohmage, providing data about your activity modes which ohmage then uploads to a central server for you to review.

Given that, open the Applications Browser and launch ohmage. If you have not previously logged in to ohmage, do so now.

Step 3: Collecting an Activity Trace

1. Mobility in Ohmage

Once you've launched ohmage and logged in, you will be presented with the ohmage dashboard. Press the icon labeled "Mobility" to continue.

2. Mobility Analytics ►

2. Mobility Analytics

The Mobility interface is separated into two screens, "Analytics" and "Control."

The screen which is currently visible, "Analytics", will eventually display a summary of your collected activity, but not until you've accumulated some data. We will revisit this screen once some data has been collected.

Click the "Control" text or swipe right on the screen to move to the next step.

◄ back 3. Mobility Control ►

3. Mobility Control

The second screen is where you'll enable and disable Mobility. The set of radio buttons at the top, labeled "Sampling Interval", control how frequently Mobility will attempt to determine your activity. "Off" indicates that Mobility is not collecting any information about your activity; "1 minute" and "5 minute" collect on 1- and 5-minute intervals, respectively.

Select "1 minute" for now, at which point the controls will briefly disable while Mobility starts up.

Once completed, you should see a "running man" icon in the notification bar, indicating that Mobility is currently collecting your activity trace. Note that you can always revisit this screen to disable Mobility whenever you like.

Once you've enabled Mobility, go ahead and continue your day, being sure to keep your phone on you. Mobility will collect and periodically upload your data, which we will review in the next step.

◄ back Reviewing Your Trace on the Phone ►

Step 4: Reviewing Your Trace on the Phone

1. Mobility Control, pt. 2

Now that you've been collecting data for a little while, let's review the Mobility Control screen again.

The area below the "Sampling Interval" control shows the modes that Mobility has detected over the past 20 minutes. It is common for the first few to be "error" as Mobility takes a little while to calibrate itself.

Activity data is only periodically uploaded to ohmage; each activity classification for a moment is called a "point." "To upload" indicates how many points are still in your phone's memory, waiting to be uploaded. "Total" indicates how many points have been collected so far. "Last upload time" display the time at which the application attempted to push the points on the phone to the server. Finally, the "Upload Now" button allows you to force the phone to upload all the points in its memory, which should cause "To Upload" to drop to zero.

Press "Upload Now", which will ensure that the server has the most recent copy of your data, then press "Analytics" or swipe to the left to continue.

2. Mobility Analytics, pt. 2 ►

2. Mobility Analytics, pt. 2

Now that we have some data, the "Analytics" screen becomes more useful. (Note that the screenshot is the result of running the app for a few days, and thus might not match your display.)

The screen is split into two main portions: a daily activity summary and a weekly summary of hours of activity per day. The last two statistics ("Average amount of activity during baseline" and "Last Week") are still works in progress and are not yet implemented.

The first portion, daily summary, shows a daily sum of the number of minutes you've spent in each of the four activity modes: still, walking, running, or driving. The second portion shows a total for each day of the number of hours you spent performing physical activity (that is, not still or driving.)

The information on this screen is intended to be a brief summary to supplement the more complete information available via the ohmage dashboard website, which we will review in the next step.

Step 5: Reviewing Your Trace on the Dashboard

1. Accessing the Dashboard

Similarly to the ohmage installation tutorial, we'll head to the ohmage website at https://play.ohmage.org.

Once you're there, enter the username and password you chose at registration and press "Log In".

2. The Dashboard ►

2. The Dashboard

Once you've logged in, you'll see the dashboard, which consists largely of controls for manipulating campaigns.

We're interested in the "Explore Data" section of the site, accessible from the navigation bar (and indicated with a gray arrow in the screenshot.)

◄ back 3. Explore Data Tab ►

3. Explore Data Tab

Once you've selected "Explore Data", you should see a tree-view on the lefthand side of the screen. Expand the last option, "Mobility", to display the available visualizations for your collected activity data.

Select "Daily Summary" from the available Mobility visualizations on the left, then click the "Date" field below to bring up a popup calendar. Click today's date, then press the "Draw Plot" button.

◄ back 4. Daily Summary View ►

4. Daily Summary View

The main content area of the page will update to display a breakdown of your day's activities.

The topmost section contains a few summarizing statistics about your day -- the amount of time you've been sedentary, ambulatory, how far you've traveled without driving, and finally how much time Mobility has been tracking total, regardless of your activity mode.

The center section is a color-coded strip that shows your activity throughout the day, with the x-axis indicating the time of day. Consult the legend on the right to determine which colors correspond to which activity modes.

The bottom section displays comparisons of your activity modes. The lefthand bottom side displays the number of meters traveled by driving versus running. The righthand bottom section displays the amount of time spent in each mode as a bar graph.

◄ back 5. Mobility Map ►

5. Mobility Map

Select "Mobility Map" from the available views, then press "Draw Plot", selecting today's date from the "Date" field if it is not already selected.

The "Mobility Map" view displays a map of the places in which activity data was gathered. The icons superimposed over each location indicate the activity mode at that location. Click an icon to display detailed information about that point, including the GPS coordinates, accuracy reading, and the time at which it was collected.

If there are too many points to display in a location, they will be "collapsed" into a cluster which displays the number of points at that place. Hovering over the cluster will display a popup that shows a summary of the four modes for that place. Zoom in to see the individual points.

◄ back 6. Temporal Summary ►

6. Temporal Summary

Select "Temporal Summary" from the available views. This view is different from the previous views -- rather than specifying a single day, you can specify a range of days over which to construct the view. This view is more useful if you want to get an overview of multiple days, but you can still use it for a single day as well. In this case, select today's date as both the start and end date.

The "Temporal Summary" shows a color-coded activity bar (identical to the one on the "Daily Summary" view) for each day in the specified range. By having multiple bars from a range of days visible on the same page, one should be able to detect patterns in activity on timescales larger than a single day.

◄ back 7. Historical Analysis ►

7. Historical Analysis

The final view is the "Historical Analysis" view. Like the "Temporal Summary" view, it is most effective when a range of dates are selected.

Given a large enough range of dates, the view displays the change in daily duration and/or distance traveled for a particular activity over time.

The top portion of the view allows you to select whether to plot duration, distance, or both. The mode selection allows you to choose a particular activity mode on which to focus. (Note that "still" is disabled when "Distance in Meters" is checked, as it is impossible to travel while still.)

The bottom portion displays a line-graph, with time on the x-axis, and the chosen metrics forming the y-axes. If both duration and distance are selected as metrics, the duration scale will be displayed on the left and the distance will be displayed on the right. Using this view, you can determine how a particular activity mode fluctuates over time.

◄ back Next Steps ►

Step 6: Next Steps

1. Review and Next Steps

In this tutorial, you learned how to install Mobility, control it to gather activity data, and review that data both on the phone and on the web dashboard. Remember that you can disable Mobility whenever you like, and that your activity data is considered sensitive and thus held in confidence.

From here, it's probably a good idea to keep Mobility running for a few days so that you can identify interesting patterns in your activity. If you find that your battery life is negatively impacted, you can change the sampling interval to 5 minutes in the Mobility configuration screen.
